Exploring the Global Phenomenon of Billie EIlish's "What was I made for?"

 

Introduction

Barbie has always been more than a doll. For generations, countless children have grown up with her as the epitome of beauty, success, and perfection. However, this image of Barbie also creates restrictive ideals. In Greta Gerwig’s 2023 film, Barbie, these limitations are brought to light as the protagonist, Stereotypical Barbie, experiences an emotional revelation in understanding the realities of womanhood (Gerwig 2023). Billie Eilish’s “What was I made for?” plays softly in the back, mirroring Barbie’s acceptance of the imperfections of real life. As Eilish herself admitted, the song originally written for a fictional character evolved into an opportunity to speak for herself. Eilish’s refusal to conform to hyperfeminine standards, her gender-neutral aesthetic, and her unfiltered emotional expression all challenge conventional images of female pop icons (Burns and Lafrance 2001). In projecting her own experiences of being criticized for her unconventionality, Billie Eilish created a relatable story of self-ambiguity and doubt. This project explores how “What was I made for?” creates a space to acknowledge emotional ambiguity and, rather than offering answers, provides reassurance and comfort.

The Critical Reading Assignment analyzes Jessica A. Holmes’s “Billie Eilish and the Feminist Aesthetics of Depression” (2023). Holmes explores how Eilish’s music and public image challenge stereotypes of the female pop star image. This article discussed Eilish’s unconventional “sad girl” persona, rooted in her unique whisper singing, oversized clothing style, and emotional transparency (Holmes 2023). Holmes’s article deepens the idea that Eilish’s quietness is not passive but a mode of agency and active empowerment. Eilish’s unintentional self-insertion into the song allows the song to resonate powerfully with its listeners. The song provides reassurance to its audience as they search for self and purpose, validating emotional and identity ambiguity as a shared experience (Holmes 2023). This analysis establishes our understanding that “What was I made for?” creates meaning not through certainty, but by acknowledging emotions and encouraging open reflection. 

This theme of personal acceptance and emotional ambiguity is further developed in the Playlist and Listening Guide, which places Eilish’s “What was I made for?” at the transitional core of a seven-song journey of self-reflection. The playlist explores the evolution from grief and loss to acceptance and empowerment. Opening up with the melancholic voices of Jeremy Zucker and Chelsea Cutler through “you were good to me”, we build toward self-reclamation, ending with Eilish’s “bad guy.” “What was I made for?” situates itself in the middle of the playlist and sits within the sense of uncertainty as the journey shifts towards self-agency from a period of heartbreak and doubt. In this context, “What was I made for?” doesn’t seek to offer an answer, but to encourage confrontation with the question itself: What was I made for? Each song in the compilation reflects a stage of personal development from an encounter with loss. As the playlist progresses, I hope listeners’ emotions become paralleled with the accelerating beats, feeling the exhilaration of self-acceptance.

With its tremendous accomplishments, “What was I made for?” materialized its impact with an Oscars win. The music review of this performance centers around the analysis of Eilish’s heartfelt Oscars performance in 2024. The performance relied primarily on a soft piano and her breathy, ASMR-like vocals. I argue that in this way, the performance becomes much more raw, as there is much more emphasis placed on Eilish’s lyricism. On this note, this review expanded on Eilish’s performance techniques, in which she imbues each word and phrase with weight and intention, pausing between phrases to allow for reflection. Contrary to the idea that performances do not contribute deeper meanings to the original song, Eilish’s Oscars performance added more emotional weight as she turned the award show into a space for communal reflection. In this sense, Eilish’s unique “quietness” is more powerful than elaborate and “loud” shows.

Eilish has spoken openly about the “war” of being a woman and how her experiences of being sexualized influenced the emotional honesty of the track (Stephan 2023). This honesty is exactly what touches its audiences. Through the exploration of “What was I made for?” as a TikTok trend, we bear witness to the cultural influence of the song, particularly regarding its reception by its young listeners. This analysis traces the song’s presence and influence across social media platforms such as TikTok and Instagram, reflecting a shift in authorship. Instead of simply consuming the song, the audience becomes active participants, remixing, editing, creating song covers, and much more. Through seeing the videos of girlhood, gender identity, nostalgia, personal growth, and female empowerment, we gain first-hand experience to understand the influence it has on its audience, who have used it as a method to retell their own stories. The song’s digital presence is a reflection of its transformation into a collective archive of lived experiences.

“What was I made for?” is not about solutions, but rather about reassurance, comfort, and placing value and strength in the idea of “not knowing.” When faced with the expectations to constantly provide answers and possess certainty, perhaps asking the question “What was I made for?” can be more important than answering it.
